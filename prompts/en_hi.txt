TECHNIQUE 1: INTERNALIZE → EXTERNALIZE (SHOW, COMPLETE, AND WRITE SILENCE)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Every internal thought, feeling, or passive description must become a physical or audible action.
The listener cannot see — make them HEAR and FEEL.
BAD: उसने मन में सोचा, "मैं क्रेवन हूँ।"
GOOD: "मैं Kraven हूँ…" उसके होंठों से धीमी गरज निकली। उसकी गरज से पूरा कमरा काँप उठा।

BAD: उसने अपनी उँगलियों पर दरवाज़ा बंद होते देखा।
GOOD: दरवाज़ा ज़ोर से उसके मुँह पर बंद हो गया।
ADVANCED RULES FOR EXTERNALIZING:
1. COMPLETE THE PHYSICAL LOOP: Don't just start a reaction, finish it. If sweat appears on a forehead, does the character wipe it nervously, or ignore it? If hands tremble, do they hide them in their pockets?
2. WRITE THE SILENCE: When a character is shocked, or alone, or when someone vanishes, do not just write "वो गायब हो गई" and move on. Write the character's silence. Where do his hands go? How does his breathing change? Does he clench his fist? Silence is an action.
3. ACTION OVER SIMILES: Do not use similes as a substitute for action. (e.g., BAD: "वो ऐसे पीछे हटी जैसे बिजली लगी हो।" GOOD: "वो झटके से पीछे हटी, उसकी साँसें तेज़ हो गईं।") Show the actual physical flinch.

BANNED: Gurmukhi, Urdu/Arabic script. Common Urdu words (ज़िंदगी, वक़्त) must be in Devanagari.
English words in Roman script: "Car rocket की तरह आगे बढ़ गई।"
Banned bookish words: तत्पश्चात, सर्वप्रथम, किंतु, परंतु, व्यक्ति, विवश, प्रतीत.
Required natural replacements: उसके बाद, सबसे पहले, लेकिन, पर, आदमी/शख्स, मजबूर, ऐसा लगा.
Pronouns: यह → ये, वह/वे → वो. Always.
RELIGIOUS/CASTE NEUTRALITY (CRITICAL):
Absolutely NO religious or caste references, analogies, or metaphors.
BANNED: कट्टर पंडित, कलयुग का राम, महाभारत, वनवास, etc.
Replace with culturally neutral, emotional equivalents (e.g., instead of "कलयुग का राम", use "अपने उसूलों का पक्का").
VOCABULARY: SIMPLE SPOKEN HINDI (NON-NEGOTIABLE):
Prefer simple, commonly spoken Hindi words. Avoid heavy/literary/rare words that a typical Hindi listener would not instantly understand.
STRICTLY BANNED COMPLEX WORDS: खस्ताहाल, भभक, झकझोरना, भयंकर उमस, नोटों की एक गड्डी (use नोटों का bundle instead), रगों में दहशत.
Use natural equivalents: टूटी-फूटी, लपटें, हिला देना, बहुत गर्मी, डर.
If in doubt, pick the word a friend would use in casual conversation.

TECHNIQUE 8: NARRATION-TO-INTERNAL-VOICE CONVERSION (MASALEDAAR SELF-TALK)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Scan the script for narration lines that would sound more alive as a character's internal voice. Convert selectively with age-appropriate, punchy self-talk that adds drama and relatability.
WHAT TO CONVERT:
Self-justifications
Cheeky observations
Frustrated reactions
Anxious thoughts
Sarcastic self-praise
Desires/fantasies
Moments of panic, guilt, or excitement
Crucial Reactions: NEVER leave a character silent in their head when they receive life-altering news or have a high-stakes conversation. Give them an internal reaction.
Comic Opportunities: Exploit awkward moments with funny internal self-talk.
WHAT NOT TO CONVERT:
Plot information ("वो तीन साल से Mumbai में रहता था")
Physical descriptions ("लड़की की dress गीली थी")
Scene-setting ("बाहर बारिश हो रही थी")
Action beats ("उसने दरवाज़ा खोला")
Lines that work perfectly fine as narration
EXISTING DIALOGUE. Never convert spoken dialogue into internal voice or indirect narration.
TONE-MATCHING & GRAMMAR (CRITICAL):
DO NOT use casual slang like "पागल है क्या तू?" in serious, life-or-death, or highly tense scenes. Match the gravity of the moment.
CONSISTENCY OF PERSON (CRITICAL): Do NOT mix 1st person ("मैं") and 2nd person ("तू") inside the same internal monologue quote. Either keep the whole thought in "मैं" (I) OR keep it entirely in "तू" (You addressing yourself).
BAD (Mixed): "मैं gentleman हूँ यार। पर बेटा, तू तो गया!"
GOOD (All 1st Person): "मैं gentleman हूँ यार। अब क्या करूँ? अरे भाई मैं तो गया!"
GOOD (All 2nd Person): "तू gentleman है यार। अब क्या करेगा? बेटा तू तो गया!"
Example 1 — Self-justification with masala:
Before: आख़िर गीले कपड़ों में सोएगी तो बीमार पड़ जाएगी। एक gentleman होने के नाते कैसे छोड़ दे उसे तकलीफ़ में?
After: उसने खुद से कहा, "गीले कपड़ों में सोएगी तो बीमार पड़ जाएगी। मैं gentleman हूँ यार, इसे ऐसे तकलीफ़ में कैसे छोड़ दूँ? पर... अगर मैंने इसके कपड़े बदले.. तो भाई, मैं तो पक्का मारा गया!"

YOUNG CHARACTERS (18-30): Casual, self-roasting, meme-worthy

"ये तूने क्या कर दिया यार?"
"भगवान बचा ले बस!"
MIDDLE-AGED CHARACTERS (30-50): Practical, world-weary, responsible

"अब इसका क्या करें?"
"भगवान, और कितना test लोगे?"

OLDER CHARACTERS (50+): Philosophical, resigned, wise
"जो होना था, हो गया।"
"अब क्या फ़र्क पड़ता है।"
"ज़िंदगी ने बहुत देख लिया।"
GENDER FLEXIBILITY:
Most self-talk lines are gender-agnostic and work for all characters
For female characters, adjust naturally:
"बेटा तू तो गया" → "बेटा तू तो गई"
"तेरी तो लग गई" → "तेरी तो लग गई"
"पागल है क्या?" → "पागल है क्या?"
Additional lines for female characters (use naturally, not forced):
"ये मैंने क्या कर दिया यार?"
"अब क्या मुँह दिखाऊँगी?"
"क्या सोच रही थी तू?"
"मर गई मैं!"
"हे भगवान, ये क्या हो गया?"
NOTE: Do NOT make female self-talk only about "लोग क्या कहेंगे" or "इज़्ज़त" — that's stereotyping. Female characters panic, self-roast, fantasize, and get frustrated the same way male characters do. Keep the emotional range identical.

Before: अगर समझाने की कोशिश करे कि ये खूबसूरत लड़की सड़क पर मिली थी, तो वो कभी नहीं मानेगी।
After: उसके दिमाग में आया, "अगर बताया कि ये सड़क पर मिली थी, तो कौन मानेगा? भाई, मेरी तो लग गई! दुनिया क्या सोचेगी मेरे बारे में?"
Example 3 — Cheeky observation:
Before: मानना पड़ेगा, नींद ज़बरदस्त आई थी। कुछ लोग सच में दुनिया के best तकिए होते हैं!
After: "क्या नींद आई यार!" वो मन ही मन मुस्कुराया। "कुछ लोग सच में दुनिया के best तकिए होते हैं।"
Example 4 — Frustrated reaction:
Before: Thank you तक नहीं बोला। कितनी rude!
After: "Thank you तक नहीं बोला!" वो बुदबुदाया। "अबे इतना किया इसके लिए, और ये treatment? कितनी rude है यार!"
Example 5 — Fantasy crashing into reality:
Before: और आख़िर में... उसके प्यार में पड़ जाएगी!
After: "और आख़िर में..." उसने imagine किया, "वो मेरे प्यार में पड़ जाएगी!" फिर खुद पर हँसा। "हाँ बेटा, सपने देखना free है।"
Example 6 — Anxiety spiral (Tense scene, NO slang):
Before: क्या वो Mumbai Police को call करेगी? एक पल के लिए हथकड़ियों की आवाज़ imagine हो गई।
After: "ये Police को call तो नहीं करेगी?" उसके पसीने छूट गए। "हे भगवान, बस इस बार बचा ले!"
QUANTITY CONTROL (CRITICAL — ANTI-FORMULA):
Convert MINIMUM 1, MAXIMUM 4 per chapter
NEVER convert in the first 3 paragraphs
NEVER convert two consecutive or nearby paragraphs
Maintain at least 6-8 paragraphs gap between conversions
Vary the TYPE across the chapter:
If first is humor → next should be anxiety/frustration
If first is self-justification → next should be panic/reaction
No two conversions of same emotional type in one chapter
MASALA BALANCE CHECK:
Don't overdo the self-roasting. (1-3 "बेटा तू तो गया" type lines max per chapter)
Mix punchy one-liners with slightly longer internal thoughts.
Match masala intensity to scene intensity (panic scene = intense/serious self-talk, casual scene = light/funny self-talk).
ATTRIBUTION VARIETY CHECK:
Never repeat the same attribution tag in the same chapter
Mix positions: sometimes attribution before quote, sometimes after

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Run these checks on every paragraph before finalizing:
CHECK 1: EM DASH BAN
Replace ALL em dashes (—) with alternative constructions.
Use commas, "और", "लेकिन", "जो", "कि" or full flowing sentences instead.
Exception: ONLY retain if it's a dialogue interruption mid-sentence (speaker cut off).
Example fix: "एक खूबसूरत चेहरा—बस inches दूर" → "एक खूबसूरत चेहरा, बस कुछ inches दूर था।"
CHECK 2: EXPLICIT VOCABULARY SCAN
Replace these English words in ALL contexts (dialogue, narration, thoughts):
"sex" → "गलत मतलब" / "कुछ गलत" / "वैसा कुछ"
"creepy" → "अजीब" / "भद्दा"
Any body-related explicit English term → Hindi euphemism
Example fix: "sex के लिए किसी को घर लाया है" → "गलत मतलब से किसी को घर लाया है"
CHECK 3: FRAGMENT CLUSTERING (NO CHOPPY SENTENCES)
If you find 2+ consecutive sentences under 4 words each, MERGE them to flow naturally:
"बिना बताए। बिना credit माँगे। Noble soul।"
→ "बिना बताए, बिना credit माँगे, एकदम noble soul।"
"देखने में अठारह-उन्नीस की।" (single fragment is OK if conversational)
"तभी झटपटाने की आवाज़ आई। और snowshoes की भी।"
→ "तभी झटपटाने और snowshoes की आवाज़ आई।"
Rule: Maximum 1 standalone fragment per 5 sentences. If more, merge using connectors.
CHECK 4: REPETITIVE RHYTHM DETECTION
Read 10 consecutive sentences. If more than 3 follow the same length/structure pattern, vary them:
If all short → combine some with "और/लेकिन/जो"
If all long → break one strategically
Goal: Natural spoken cadence, not poetic staccato.

TECHNIQUE 1: INTERNALIZE → EXTERNALIZE (SHOW, COMPLETE, WRITE SILENCE)

Flag any line where an emotion, thought, or feeling is stated rather than shown through a physical or audible action. ALSO flag incomplete physical reactions, missing silence, or similes acting as substitutes for action.

PASS (Showing): डर के मारे Huddleston की पैंट गीली हो गई।
FAIL (Incomplete Loop): उसके माथे पर पसीना आ गया। (The scene moves on without the character wiping it, ignoring it, or reacting to it).

TECHNIQUE 7: CONTENT INTEGRITY — NO DROPPED PLOT & NO SUMMARIZED DIALOGUE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Compare the dramatized script against the original script. This is the most critical check.
DIALOGUE PRESERVATION:
FAIL: The source had "मैं आ रही हूँ!", but the writer changed it to: श्रुति बाहर से बोली कि वो आ रही है।
PASS: The dialogue is kept in direct quotes: "अंदर हो? मैं आ रही हूँ!"
NO DROPPED BEATS:
FAIL: A character's internal realization, a minor action, or a logical transition from the source is missing in the dramatized version.
ZERO HALLUCINATION (FACTUAL FIDELITY):
FAIL: The writer added a family member (e.g., "अगर माँ को पता चलता तो वो ज़िंदा गाड़ देतीं"), a specific number ("आठ सेकंड"), or a backstory not present in the original text.
Check: Was ANY direct dialogue turned into indirect narration? Were facts/mothers invented? Were plot points skipped? Flag it immediately.

TECHNIQUE 8: AUDIO FORMAT & SENSITIVITY COMPLIANCE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Flag any of these specific violations:
A. RELIGIOUS/CASTE REFERENCES: Banned terms like कट्टर पंडित, कलयुग का राम, महाभारत, वनवास, etc.
B. BANNED COMPLEX VOCABULARY: खस्ताहाल, भभक, झकझोरना, भयंकर उमस, नोटों की एक गड्डी, रगों में दहशत.
C. ONOMATOPOEIA: Comic-book sounds like खट-खट, धड़ाम, ठाँ-ठाँ. (Must be described naturally).
D. SFX tags still present: "(SFX: ...)" or "[SFX: ...]" — must be rewritten as prose.
E. Urdu/Arabic script used: (e.g., طرف, کیا) — must be Devanagari.
F. Gurmukhi script used: (e.g., ਨੇ) — must be Devanagari.
G. Banned bookish words present: तत्पश्चात, सर्वप्रथम, किंतु, परंतु, व्यक्ति, विवश, प्रतीत.
H. Bookish pronouns: यह or वह/वे instead of ये and वो.
I. Narrator and dialogue blended in the same sentence (not in separate paragraphs).
J. Visual-only details that serve no audio purpose (e.g., detailed room décor, clothing color descriptions with no emotional relevance).

TECHNIQUE 9: NARRATION-TO-INTERNAL-VOICE CONVERSION AUDIT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Check whether narration-to-internal-voice conversions (masaledaar self-talk) are applied correctly.
CRUCIAL REACTIONS (NEW):
FAIL: A character receives life-altering news or has a high-stakes conversation but has zero internal self-talk/reaction to it in the scene.
GRAMMAR & PERSPECTIVE (CRITICAL):
FAIL: Mixing 1st person ("मैं") and 2nd person ("तू") inside the same internal thought quote.
Example FAIL: उसने खुद से कहा, "मैं gentleman हूँ यार। पर बेटा, तू तो गया!"
PASS: Keeping the perspective strictly consistent (All "मैं" OR All "तू").
Example PASS (1st Person): उसने खुद से कहा, "मैं gentleman हूँ यार। अब क्या करूँ? अरे भाई मैं तो गया!"
Example PASS (2nd Person): उसने खुद से कहा, "तू gentleman है यार। अब क्या करेगा? बेटा तू तो गया!"
TONE-MATCHING:
FAIL: Using "पागल है क्या तू?" in a highly tense, life-or-death scene.
PASS: Self-talk tone matches the gravity of the scene.
QUANTITY:
FAIL: Zero conversions in the entire chapter, or more than 4.
FAIL: Two conversions in consecutive or nearby paragraphs (must have 6-8 paragraph gap).
FAIL: A conversion appears in the first 3 paragraphs.
PASS: 1-4 conversions spread across the chapter with proper spacing.
VARIETY:
FAIL: Two conversions of the same emotional type (e.g., both humor, both panic).
FAIL: Same attribution tag used twice in one chapter (e.g., "उसने मन ही मन सोचा" repeated).
PASS: Different emotional tones and varied attribution tags across conversions.
AGE-APPROPRIATENESS:
FAIL: Young character (18-30) using philosophical/resigned self-talk meant for older characters.
FAIL: Older character (50+) using Gen-Z/meme-worthy self-talk.
PASS: Self-talk tone matches the character's age bracket.
GENDER:
FAIL: Female character self-talk limited only to "log kya kahenge" or "izzat" themes (stereotyping).
PASS: Female characters show the same emotional range as male characters (panic, self-roast, frustration, humor).
WHAT SHOULD NOT BE CONVERTED:
FAIL: Plot information, physical descriptions, scene-setting, or action beats converted to self-talk.
PASS: Only self-justifications, cheeky observations, frustrated reactions, anxious thoughts, or moments of panic/guilt/excitement are converted.
Check: Are conversions well-spaced, grammatically clean, varied, age-appropriate, and applied only to eligible narration? Did they miss a crucial reaction? Flag violations.

TECHNIQUE 10: FRAGMENTATION & LINGUISTIC QUALITY AUDIT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Check for linguistic quality issues that hurt audio listening experience.
EM DASH OVERUSE:
FAIL: Em dashes (—) used for routine clause-joining or pacing (e.g., "एक खूबसूरत चेहरा—बस inches दूर").
PASS: Em dashes used ONLY for dialogue interruptions (cut-offs). All other em dashes replaced with commas, "और", "लेकिन", "जो", "कि", or full sentences.
EXPLICIT VOCABULARY:
FAIL: English words like "sex", "creepy", or body-related explicit English terms present in dialogue, narration, or thoughts.
PASS: All such words replaced with Hindi euphemisms ("गलत मतलब", "अजीब", "भद्दा", etc.).
FRAGMENT CLUSTERING (CLAUDE-STYLE):
FAIL: 2+ consecutive sentences under 4 words each (e.g., "बिना बताए। बिना credit माँगे। Noble soul।").
PASS: Consecutive short fragments merged using connectors.
REPETITIVE RHYTHM:
FAIL: 3+ consecutive sentences following the exact same length/structure pattern.
Check: Scan for em dash overuse, explicit English vocabulary, fragment clusters, and repetitive rhythm patterns. Flag each violation with the exact problematic line.
